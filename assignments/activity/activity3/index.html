Activity 4

The following activity is aimed to give you some practice with exploring data and running a linear regression on your own using statistical software. You are welcome to use any statistical software you wish and you are also free to work in groups of up to 3 for this assignment. If you work in groups, please submit one completed activity per group on ICON. Please make sure to add everyone’s name to the submission, this can be a comment on ICON or on the document itself.

What to turn in? Please turn in a document that contains answers to the following questions and also please include any relevant statistics/figures that support your answer. Upload the final document to ICON.

Due around November 30th, 2021. No penalty for late submissions as long as it is submitted by December 12th.

Data

The data for this activity is pumpkin data from 2021 for those growing giant pumpkins, original data from Big Pumpkins.

Note: Use the data linked here or posted to the IDAS. The data can be obtained in csv format. A short description for each attribute is as follows.

Attribute Name Description
place Place of pumpkin based on weight (ie, rank)
weight_lbs Pumpkin weight, in lbs
name Name of grower
city City of grower
state_prov State or Province of grower
country Country of grower
gpc_site Site pumpkin was weighed
seed_mother Seed of pumpkin
pollinator_father Pumpkin pollinator
ott Over the top of the pumpkin measurement, in inches
est_weight Estimated weight, in lbs
pct_chart The percent on chart
us_dummy A dummy/indicator attribute, 1 = United States grower, 0 = otherwise
us_region A categorical attribute, west, south, northeast, midwest, other (other is outside United States locations). Regions were coded based on Census Bureau designated regions (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_regions_of_the_United_States)

Guiding Question

  1. How much variation is explained in the pumpkin weight (weight_lbs) by the over the top pumpkin measurement? a. After controlling for pumpkin over the top measurement, is there any differences between United States growers compared to growers from other countries?

Questions

  1. Explore the research questions descriptively. What are key messages based on the descriptive analysis?

  2. Fit a linear regression model to explore the first research question. Summarize the key messages/takeaways from the results of this regression model.

  3. Fit a second linear regression model to explore the subquestion above. Summarize the key messages/takeaways from the results of this regression model.

  4. Fit the third linear regression model that includes an interaction between the two primary attributes of interest from the research questions.

  5. Finally, instead of comparing United States vs other countries, try a new series of models equivalent to #3 and #4 that break the United States by their region (ie, use us_region). a. Are all of these models nested? b. Perform the appropriate model comparison to identify the best fitting model.

  6. Is the added complexity of splitting the United States into specific regions justified? Why or why not?
